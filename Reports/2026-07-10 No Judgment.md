### No Judgment
- It is widely accepted that having an LLM judge its own product is a bad practice. LLMs already have directives to create things which they believe to be 'worthy' so asking it to check its own output is like asking a child if they think their room is clean.
- Asking the same LLM to cite sources and increasing the resources available to it may help correct some hallucinations by adding knowledge to the context; however, the discernment of different values and approaches is governed by different weights baked into the training prior to release.
- Always using a family of judge_model different than the one which created the output is a good start but it may be more meaningful to gather outputs from multiple LLM sources. In other words, if you pass Claude a paper it may evaluate it to be good or bad. If you pass Claude a paper that Claude made then the evaluation will be heavily bias and may mean nothing at all. If Claude, Grok, Gemini, GPT, DeepSeek, etc. all evaluate the paper to be good then you can have more confidence in the assessment.
- A more time consuming approach would involve testing and benchmarking the exact version of LLM: By passing in synthetic prompts that you know will produce positive or negative assessments you can fine tune where that particular model draws the line and better gauge the responses. An alternative to this would be asking for a breakdown of assessments by quantifiable factors to find what qualities and characteristics the model may put weight in while making its decision. The downside of this approach would be the long process of assessing the LLM against your needs.
